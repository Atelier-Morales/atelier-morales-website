PEREGRINOS (Pilgrims) is the most recent series of photographs of the duo Atelier Morales. The works represent the romantic need of human beings to follow and reach their idols wherever there is a historic landmark.

Over time mankind has idealized mythical or real heroes and identified with their allure. Today idols such as writers, actors, musicians, politicians and legendary or charismatic figures are revered all over the world. Pilgrims go to fascinating places to look for the poetic essence of someone who left songs, books, films, ideas, art and good will as a cultural heritage to mankind.

Thus the Atelier Morales’ photographs capture pilgrimages to where Charlie Chaplin, El Che, Frida and Diego, Lady Di, Gainsbourg, Hemmingway or Lennon can be found, or to a cemetery where an anonymous mother grants miracles.

An earlier series, Viaje Equinoccial, Homenaje a A. Von Humboldt y A. Bompland, has had an important curatorial acclaim and refers to the traces left by historical travelers and tourism today in a globalized world. Atelier Morales was selected to participate at the Third Beijing International Art Biennial, China, 2008 with works from this series. All ten pieces were presented in a collective at the Schrin Kunsthalle, Frankfurt, 2008 and in a solo show at the Heidelberger Kunstverein, 2007. With photography as a mediating tool and using poetic language and images, the duo’s artistic work is conceptual and refers to history and memory.

Collective exhibitions include:
Artcurial, heartgalerie, Paris, 2009;
Viaje Equinoccial (Equinoctial Journey), X Havana Biennial, 2009;
Killing time, Exit Art, New York, 2007;
La Huella Múltiple, colateral to the IX Bienal de La Habana, 2005;
Beyond Delirious, CIFO, Miami, 2005;
Futuribles, curated by Rosa Martínez, ARCO, Madrid, 2004;
Space and Subjectivity, curated by Lauri Firstenberg, Prague Biennale 1, 2003
