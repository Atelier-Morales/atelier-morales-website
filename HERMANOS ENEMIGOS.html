Hermanos Enemigos (Brothers/enemies)

Hermanos Enemigos (Brothers/enemies) is a serie by Atelier Morales that addresses the themes of greed, violence, hate and envy between brothers.

It stretches from the family privacy to wars and massacres between peoples.

Examples are as old as the story of Cain and Abel and as actual as the ever going conflicts between Israel and Palestine; Fatha and Hamas in Gaza, Catholics and Protestants in Northern Ireland; Cubans in Miami and Havana; Basque separatists and Spaniards; Uigures and the Han in Xinjiang; Tutsis and Hutus in Ruanda; Sindis and Sijs in India, Serbian and Albanians in Kosovo, pachtounes and mujahidin’s in Afghanistan; radical and moderates pro-Islamic in Algeria among many others.

With each image there is a myth that has been updated by the artists, Juan Luis Morales and Teresa Ayuso, who conform the duo Atelier Morales.

Hermanos Enemigos (Brothers/enemies) is a very touching and humane work of investigation and art, based on moral and history. It makes us reflect upon our values an actions from ancient to contemporary time, as well as consider that evil exists because goodness and righteousness exist as well. The Atelier Morales images, based on legends and myths, are poetic, beautiful and dramatic.


Text by Atelier Morales in collaboration with Nina Menocal
