Adrift patrimony: Sugar refineries

The Sugar refineries series (2002-2004, photography and gouache) by the duo Atelier Morales is an homage to the French painter and lithographer Eduardo Laplante, who arrived in Cuba in 1849. Laplante made 38 lithographs to illustrate Justo Cantero's Vistas de los principales ingenios de Cuba* (1857).  This incredible bibliographic material is one of the most impressive Cuban books of the 19th century and equally, one of the most beautifully printed matters from the Americas. The landscapes represented in the illustrations juxtapose the sugar industry with details from everyday life in the plantations of Trinidad, Matanzas and Havana.

The lithographs were collectable items and the book became one of the most rare and valuable Cuban archives. Today Vistas de los principales ingenios de Cuba holds a larger significance. Two of the editions mysteriously disappeared from the Palacio de Junco in the Museum of Matanzas in 1993 and since then, works of art, archeological and decorative pieces, books and documents have disappeared from all national Cuban museums. This sense of loss is explored in the work, Adrift patrimony, by Atelier Morales.

Los ingenios:
Almost immediately after the sugar mills were closed in 2002, Teresa and Juan Luis roamed the old locations of Cuba and photographed landscapes where you can see sugar cane plantations, sugar mills, machinery and abandoned interiors. They visited the now closed sugar refineries, 150 years after they were illustrated in the Laplante lithographs. Atelier Morales exercise a critical, poetic reinterpretation of the sugar disaster in Cuba today. The ruins resemble surrealist art objects in a typical Cuban landscape, and the compositions are based on the original renderings of Laplante. Utilizing contemporary technology, they recreate a romanticized engraving that reflects on lost patrimony in contemporary Cuba. In the 19th century, the intention of the engravings was to celebrate the incredible technological advancements achieved by the sugar industry in Cuba. Today, however, Atelier Morales revises the most important plantations that have been victimized by the international markets’ sugar de-evaluation and the decades of inefficiency exercised by the socialist regime in Cuba.

Los Ingenios consists of 25 images, in editions of 10 each (with the exception of two images in editions of 15), signed and painted with gouache to illustrate why each one is considered an original.

Nina Menocal, 2004

*Cantero, Justo Germán (1857): Los Ingenios.  “Colección de Vistas de los principales ingenios de Azucar de la isla de Cuba”.  38 lithographs by Eduardo Laplante.  La Habana:  Print in lithograph by Luis Marquier, 1857.  (Justo Canetero was a doctor and Cuban writer, as well as a sugar lord and patron of the arts)
